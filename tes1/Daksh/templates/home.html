AI-enabled Phishing Links Detection and Alert System

Daksh is a cutting-edge program that will transform the way phishing URLs are checked for corruption or dangerous material. Daksh can identify and categorize malformed URLs without relying on centralized data by leveraging the power of federated learning. This technique of continual learning guarantees that the application is always up to date with the newest URL dangers while protecting user privacy and data security. This article delves into the underlying ideas, federated learning implementation, and the possible influence of Daksh on cybersecurity.

When a URL is modified, it redirects people to undesired and potentially hazardous websites. These tainted URLs frequently trick visitors into disclosing personal information, installing malware, or falling prey to phishing attempts. Daksh's goal is to find and classify such URLs by analysing their content, structure, and behaviour.

Daksh's strength resides in its capacity to constantly adapt from new and developing threats. As consumers visit multiple URLs on a regular basis, the decentralised models continually acquire data to enhance their accuracy. The global model is updated on a regular basis by pooling these revised models from individual devices. Daksh maintains current with the ever-changing URL threat landscape because this seamless learning process is powered by federated learning.

About Us

URLs serve as gates to information and services as the internet becomes more embedded into our daily lives. Regrettably, the internet is also afflicted by a variety of security dangers, such as tainted URLs that route visitors to harmful websites. To protect consumers from potential intrusions, strong URL corruption detection systems have become critical.

Daksh is a game-changing tool that successfully detects malformed URLs by leveraging the power of federated learning, a decentralised approach to machine learning. Unlike traditional approaches that need centralised data stores, Daksh employs a distributed strategy that protects user privacy while assuring continual development through continuous learning.

In today's digital environment, privacy is a top priority. Daksh solves this challenge by using federated learning to take a privacy-first strategy. The data of users remains on their devices, and the central server never has access to raw data. Furthermore, the local models are encrypted during aggregation to avoid data leaking. Daksh guarantees that users' browsing history and personal information remain protected.

Daksh deployment provides various benefits to individuals and the larger cybersecurity landscape: a. Real-time Threat Detection: Daksh's continuous learning guarantees that new URL risks are detected immediately, providing real-time protection against corruption and malicious redirection. b. Lower Bandwidth Consumption: Federated learning decreases the requirement for data transmission to the central server, preserving bandwidth and improving user experience. c. Device Diversity: Individuals' usage of a wide range of devices adds to a richer and more complete dataset, which improves the accuracy of Daksh's global model. d. Cost-effectiveness: Daksh avoids the requirement for costly centralised infrastructure by utilising users' current devices for model training

WHY ARE WE USING FEDRATED LEARNING?

Federated learning is a machine learning approach in which model training takes happen on decentralised devices rather than a central server, such as user cellphones or laptops. It allows Daksh to learn from a large network of devices while protecting user data privacy
